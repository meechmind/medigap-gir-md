What are Guaranteed Issue Rights?

Specific times when Medicare beneficiaries can purchase Medigap policies without medical underwriting, regardless of health status or pre-existing conditions.1

Critical Protection

GIR ensures access to supplemental coverage during qualifying life events—protecting beneficiaries from being denied based on their health.

Legal Framework

1

Federal Foundation

Baseline guaranteed issue rights established by federal law.2

2

Maryland Implementation

State adopts federal protections through regulation and statute.3

3

Maryland Enhancements

Additional protections beyond federal requirements, including the annual Birthday Rule.4

GIR Protections

When a beneficiary is entitled to guaranteed issue rights, insurers must:

  • Accept applications for specified Medigap plans without denial or conditioning based on health status5
  • No price discrimination based on health status, claims experience, or medical condition6
  • No pre-existing condition exclusions if beneficiary has at least 6 months of creditable coverage7

Critical Limitations

Specific Plans Only

GIR applies only to certain Medigap plans depending on which event triggered the right.8

63-day Window

GIR has strict time limits, typically ending 63 days after the start of the guaranteed issue period.9

Evidence Required

Beneficiaries must submit evidence of the qualifying event with their application.10

Guaranteed Issue Triggers

Federal and Maryland law create guaranteed issue rights when specific qualifying events occur. Understanding these triggers is essential for counseling beneficiaries.

  • Birthday Rule

  • Loss of Other Health Coverage

  • Trial Rights

  • Insurance Carrier Misconduct or Insolvancy

  • Network Changes

Maryland's Birthday Rule

Annual Opportunity to Switch

Maryland policyholders can switch to a Medigap policy of equal or lesser benefits without medical underwriting. The window starts on their birthday and lasts for 30 days.32

Carriers must notify policyholders of this right 30 to 60 days before their birthday.

Loss of Other Health Coverage

Policyholders have a GIR if they lose certain types of coverage through no fault of their own. This period generally lasts for 63 days from the date coverage ends.

1
Loss of Employer Coverage
Group Health Plan that covered Medicare cost-sharing (seconday to Medicare) terminates or stops providing health benefits.11
2
Plan Termination or Exit
Medigap policy, Medicare Advantage (MA) plan, or PACE program ends its coverage, leaves the Medicare program, or leaves the service area.12
2
Moving
Beneficiary moves out of the service area of their Medicare Advantage, Medicare SELECT, or PACE plan.12,15

"Trial Rights"

There are specific triggers designed to allow beneficiaries to try Medicare Advantage (MA) without permanently losing access to Medigap.

1
First MA/PACE at Age 65
Joined MA/PACE once first eligible at 65 then disenrolled within 12 months.19
2
First MA/Cost Plan/PACE from Medigap
Terminated Medigap policy, enrolled in MA/cost/PACE for first time, then disenrolled within 12 months.18

"For Cause"

Triggers based on the conduct or status of the insurance carrier.

1
Bankruptcy
The insurance carrier goes bankrupt17
2
Breach of Contract
The carrier breaches a material term of the contract17
3
Fraud or Misrepresentation
The carrier or insurance producer (agent) materially misrepresented the provisions of the plan in marketing it to the individual, or committed fraud17,13

Scenario: Employer Terminates Retiree Plan

✓ GIR Applies

Situation

A 70-year-old retiree receives supplemental health benefits through her former employer. The employer notifies her in March that the retiree health plan will terminate June 30.

Outcome

She has guaranteed issue rights to purchase a Medigap policy to replace her coverage.21

Trigger 1: Loss of employer-sponsored supplemental coverage

Scenario: MA Plan Discontinues in County

✓ GIR Applies

Situation

A 68-year-old has been in a Medicare Advantage HMO for three years. In September, he receives notice the plan will no longer be offered in his county starting January 1.

Outcome

He has guaranteed issue rights to purchase a Medigap policy instead of choosing a different MA plan.22

Trigger 2: Medicare Advantage plan termination or discontinuation

Scenario: Trial Right After First MA Enrollment

✓ GIR Applies

Situation

A 67-year-old had Medigap Plan G for two years. In January, she switched to a Medicare Advantage plan for the first time (attracted by $0 premium). By August, she's frustrated with network restrictions.

Outcome

She can return to Original Medicare with a Medigap policy using her trial right.23

Trigger 8: Trial right—first-time enrollment in MA after leaving Medigap

Scenario: Trial Right at Age 65

✓ GIR Applies

Situation

A woman turns 65 in February, enrolls in Parts A & B with a Medicare Advantage plan starting March 1. She never had Medigap. By November, she prefers Original Medicare with Medigap.

Outcome

She can disenroll from MA and purchase Medigap using her trial right (within 12 months of MA enrollment).24

Trigger 9: Trial right—first MA enrollment at age 65

When GIR Does NOT Apply

✗ No GIR

Voluntary Termination Before Plan Ends

A 72-year-old retiree has employer-sponsored supplemental coverage. She decides to drop it voluntarily in June to purchase Medigap, even though the employer plan is not terminating.

Why Not

GIR requires the employer plan to terminate, not voluntary departure by the beneficiary.25

When GIR Does NOT Apply

✗ No GIR

Second or Third MA Enrollment

A 69-year-old had Medigap, tried MA for 10 months (first time), returned to Medigap using trial right. Two years later, she tries MA again and after 6 months wants to return to Medigap.

Why Not

Trial right applies only to first-time enrollment in MA/cost plan/PACE.26

When GIR Does NOT Apply

✗ No GIR

Under 65, Disabled, Outside Initial Window

A 45-year-old became eligible for Medicare due to disability two years ago. She enrolled in Part B at that time but did not purchase Medigap. Now she wants guaranteed issue.

Why Not

GIR for individuals under 65 applies only during the 6-month period following Part B enrollment.27

#1

Multiple Windows Beyond Initial Enrollment

Maryland law creates guaranteed issue rights beyond the initial 6-month open enrollment period when qualifying events occur—plus annually during the 30 days after a policyholder's birthday (Birthday Rule).28

#2

Timing is Strictly Enforced

Applications submitted after the 63-day window do not receive guaranteed issue protections.29

#3

Plans Available Vary by Trigger

Most Triggers

Access to plans A, B, C, F (including high-deductible F), K, or L

First MA at Age 65

Access to any plan offered by any issuer

Trial-right triggers may entitle beneficiary to their previous policy if available from the same issuer.30

#4

Documentation is Required

No evidence, no guaranteed issue.

Beneficiaries must submit evidence of the termination, disenrollment, or Part D enrollment date with their Medigap application to qualify for GIR protections. Without documentation, the applicant is not an "eligible individual."31

#5

Maryland's Birthday Rule

Annual Switching Opportunity

Starting July 1, 2023, Maryland policyholders can switch to a Medigap policy of equal or lesser benefits during the 30 days following their birthday each year, without medical underwriting.32

This is in addition to federal guaranteed issue rights.

#6

Special Protections for Under 65

6-Month Window

Individuals under 65 eligible for Medicare due to disability have GIR to purchase Plan A (and Plan D if offered to 65+) during 6 months following Part B enrollment.

Premium Limits

Premiums for Plan A cannot exceed the average premium paid by policyholders age 65+ in Maryland for the same plan.33
